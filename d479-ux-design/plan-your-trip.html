    Tips and Facts About Taniti

    Important things to know so you have a safe and hassle-free visit.

    Customs: Taniti requires all visitors to have a passport.

    Currency: The US dollar is the offical Currency of Taniti though many businesses also accept euros and yen, as well as major credit cards. Several banks are available to exchange currencies.

    Language: Younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in urban areas. On the other hand, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially among older residents.

    Power Outlets: Power outlet standards are the same as in the United States: 120 volts/15 amps with 3 prongs for a safe grounded connection.

    Holidays: Taniti observes most of the world's favorite holidays, as well as several more unique to Taniti. Many tourist attractions and restaurants close on these holidays, so plan accordingly.

    Grocery Stores: Taniti features two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and a 24-hour convenience store.

    Medical Services: Taniti has the main Taniti General Hospital, where the employee base speaks a variety of languages, and several clinics.

    Alcohol: Serving or selling alcohol is prohibited between midnight and 9 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is 18.

    Crime: Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but like with any popular tourist destination, be aware of pickpockets.
